November 2023

Smoothing manuscripts published

Our two-part manuscript using nonlinear ensemble triangular transport for smoothing has just been published in the open-access Journal of Computational Physics X! If you are curious, you can check out the papers here and here. In Part 1, we discuss how many well-known linear smoothing algorithms emerge as special cases of a general ensemble transport smoother. In Part 2, we discuss generalizations to nonlinear smoothing updates, and demonstrate their performance in nonlinear and chaotic settings. Many thanks to my co-authors Ricardo Baptista (Caltech), Dennis McLaughlin (MIT), and Youssef Marzouk (MIT)!

EnKF Workshop in Norheimsund

I have just returned from the 2023 EnKF Workshop in Norheimsund, Norway. During that workshop, I present my recent work on the efficient adaptation of nonlinear transport maps both as an oral talk and an accompanying poster. In brief, our methods allow us to find efficient and parsimonious transport maps for nonlinear data assimilation, which furthermore reveal and exploit conditional independence structure in the system. This permits a very efficient form of adaptive localization. My poster and a recording of my talk can be found in the corresponding sections of my website. I met many interesting people and learned a lot about different areas of data assimilation research at that conference, and can only recommend attending!

November 2022

Ensemble transport smoothing

The pre-prints for my twinned manuscripts about ensemble transport smoothers (EnTS) are now available on arXiv! In Part 1 we introduce the general algorithm, and demonstrate how different existing smoothing algorithms arise as linear special cases of a general nonlinear EnTS. In Part 2, we explore its performance in various nonlinear and chaotic test cases, and match or outperform existing state-of-the-art smoothing algorithms. This work in collaboration with Ricardo Baptista (Caltech), Dennis McLaughlin (MIT), and Youssef Marzouk (MIT) is currently under review in the Journal of Computational Physics. You can access the preprints by following the links above.

October 2021

Poster at AGU Fall meeting 2021

I will present some of my work at MIT as a poster in the session on Advances in Data Assimilation, Predictability and Uncertainty Quantification at the AGU Fall Meeting in New Orleans this December. This poster will cover the use of transport maps for smoothing, a data assimilation technique similar to filtering, which extends the inference update back to all previous states as well. We find that transport maps offer a unifying perspective on smoothing, from which diverse linear smoothing algorithms can be derived as special cases. We also report nonlinear smoothing results, and show that when it comes to nonlinearity, not all linear smoothers translate equally well to their nonlinear transport extensions.


June 2021

Möbius

My lockdown side-project has just been published in Water Resources Research! In this manuscript, we explore the analytic element method, a highly-efficient, grid-free groundwater modelling technique based on complex algebra. We expanded the set of classic analytic elements with a regional flow element based on a Möbius transformation, which allows us to induce curving, diverging, and converging regional flow without the need for conventional boundary conditions.

The manuscript comes with a Python toolbox which permits the modular assembly of an analytic element model, with all the more nasty math happening behind the scenes. This toolbox also has an in-build uncertainty quantification routine based on Differential Evolution Monte Carlo.
